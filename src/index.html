Chester Bennington

The man who touched the hearts of billions

Here's a time line of Chester Bennington's life:

  • 1976 - Born in Phoenix, Arizona
  • 1993 - He became part of his first band ‘Sean Dowdell and His Friends?’
  • Later in 1993 - He joined the band ‘Grey Daze’, however he was not happy being part of the band as he was not given due credit.
  • 1998 - Left 'Grey Daze'.
  • 2000 - Joined the band ‘Linkin Park’, they also released their debut album, ‘Hybrid Theory,’ for which he provided the vocals. This album soon became the best selling album of the year.
  • 2003 - He provided vocals for the ‘Linkin Park’ album ‘Meteora,’ which was released under ‘Warner Bros. Records.’ The album earned worldwide recognition and peaked at number one positions in both the US and UK charts. It also peaked at number two in Australia immediately after it was released.
  • 2005 - He created his own rock band named ‘Dead by Sunrise.’
  • 2006 - He played the role of a pharmacy customer in the American action film ‘Crank.’
  • 2007 - Released his third album ‘Minutes to Midnight’ selling over 600,000 copies in its first week and peaking at the top spot on ‘Billboard’ charts.
  • 2010 - He provided vocals for Linkin Park’s fourth album ‘A Thousand Suns’ which was a multi-concept album, highlighting the concept of nuclear warfare.
  • 2010 - He appeared in a minor role in the 3D horror film ‘Saw 3D.’
  • 2012 - He featured as the lead singer for Linkin Park’s album ‘Living Things,’ which was one among the band’s bestselling albums.
  • 2017 - Chester was found dead in his home. He had committed suicide by hanging. It was reported that he was very upset following the death of his friend Chris Cornell. Bennington committed suicide on what would have been Cornell's 53rd birthday.
